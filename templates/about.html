About

Fernanda is a software engineer based out of Minneapolis, Minnesota with a passion for tech, diversity, and sustainability.

¡Hola! I'm Fernanda!

A biochemistry grad turned software engineer seeking my next opportunity!

Now, I'll be honest about the reason for making the career switch to software engineering: I had no clue that computer science existed until my junior year of college, but by then it was just too late to make the switch.

Despite being a little late to the coding game in comparison to some, I do have a fierce tenacity to accomplish my goals at all costs. Growing up the eldest daughter of Mexican immigrants in America meant prioritizing all matters requiring English translation—bills, taxes, appointments—even if it meant starting homework at midnight. To top it all off, growing up a poor person of color in white, rural America in the early 2000s meant a guaranteed lack of support. I was one of eight colored kids my age to grow up together in a trailer park, but the only one to receive a college education. This shows that while I may not have a computer science degree, I refuse to let anything get in the way of my goals.

Most recently, I was 1 of 15 out of 1200 applicants selected to receive a full-ride scholarship to Hackbright Academy's full stack bootcamp. I completed the bootcamp in December of 2022, but have remained dedicated to learning. Right now I'm working as a freelance developer. I'm currently redoing a website for a client who is as passionate about helping underserved individuals as I am about working towards changing the ratio in tech! The second project I am working on is an open source project alongside other Latina developers. Given that less than 2% of engineers are Latinas, we envision it as a platform where Latina software engineers can go to connect, discuss projects, receive advice/feedback, and most of all, find a community! I also recently completed a bootcamp with Girls Who Code that was sponsored by Autodesk where I programmed alongside senior engineers from Autodesk, practiced technical interview skills, and collaborated with fellow Girls Who Code students. Last but not least, to enrich my knowledge, I have been taking a Mathematics of Machine Learning course on Coursera and was recently accepted into Code the Dream's React and Node.js courses!

In my free time I love to volunteer with local organizaitons in the Twin Cities like Twin Cities Pet Rescue, Minnesota Immigrant Rights Action Coalition (MIRAC), and Open Streets Minneapolis. I'm also a huge nature lover and in the summer you'll find me partaking in pretty much all outdoor activities such as hiking, biking, paddle boarding, and walking around the many Minnesota lakes with my one-year-old mini Australian shepherd, Kovu! When not outside, I'm usually inside reading, working on a project, working out, or whipping up a tasty vegetarian meal!
